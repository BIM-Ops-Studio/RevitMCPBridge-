### Cleanouts (FPC 708)

#### Spacing
- **Horizontal drains**: Every 100' max
- **Change of direction**: At 45° or greater
- **Building drain/sewer**: At building line
- **Base of each stack**: Required

#### Size
- Cleanout = same size as pipe (up to 4")
- Pipes > 4": 4" cleanout minimum

#### Clearance
- **3" and smaller**: 12" in front
- **4" and larger**: 18" in front
- **Concealed cleanouts**: Accessible cover required

### Vent Termination (FPC 903)
- **Height above roof**: 6" minimum (12" if snow)
- **Distance from vertical surface**: 10' min if wall extends 1' above vent
- **Distance from openable window**: 10' horizontal, 3' above
- **Distance from air intake**: 10' minimum

- Bathroom group can wet vent through lavatory
- Horizontal wet vent: 2" minimum
- Max 2 fixtures per branch

- **Prohibited**: Cannot be sole vent for building
- **Location**: 4" above horizontal drain
- **Access**: Required for inspection/replacement
- **Height**: 6" above flood level rim

### Backflow Prevention (FPC 608)

#### Required Devices

| Application | Device |
|-------------|--------|
| **Irrigation systems** | Reduced pressure zone (RPZ) |
| **Fire sprinkler** | Double check valve assembly |
| **Boiler makeup** | RPZ |
| **Chemical dispensing** | Air gap or RPZ |
| **Hose bibb** | Vacuum breaker |
| **Submerged inlet** | Air gap |
| **Lab equipment** | RPZ or air gap |
| **Swimming pool fill** | Air gap or ASSE 1056 |

#### Air Gap Requirements
- **Min gap**: 2× pipe diameter
- **Never less than**: 1"
- **Measured from**: Flood level rim to outlet

#### Vacuum Breaker Heights
- **Atmospheric vacuum breaker**: 6" above flood level
- **Pressure vacuum breaker**: 12" above downstream use
- **Spill-resistant VB**: 6" above flood level

### Hot Water System

#### Temperature Requirements
| Application | Max Temp |
|-------------|----------|
| Showers/tubs (residential) | 120°F |
| Showers/tubs (public) | 120°F |
| Lavatory (public) | 110°F |
| General service | 120°F |
| Healthcare | 110°F point of use |

#### Mixing Valves (FPC 424)
- **Showers**: Anti-scald required (ASSE 1016)
- **Bathtubs**: Pressure-balanced or thermostatic
- **Public lavatories**: Point-of-use mixing valve

#### Water Heater Installation (FPC 504)
- **T&P relief valve**: Required, 3/4" min
- **Discharge**: To floor drain or exterior
- **Discharge termination**: 6" - 24" above floor/grade
- **Expansion tank**: Required for closed systems
- **Seismic strapping**: Required (per local jurisdiction)
- **Pan**: Required in locations where leakage causes damage
- **Pan drain**: 3/4" minimum

## Florida-Specific Requirements

### Hurricane/Flood Zone

#### Equipment Above Flood Level
- **Water heaters**: Install above BFE
- **Water softeners**: Above BFE
- **Pressure tanks**: Above BFE or flood-resistant

#### Pipe Materials (Corrosion)
- **Coastal areas**: PVC, CPVC, or coated metal
- **Avoid**: Galvanized in salt air environments
- **Underground**: PVC, ductile iron, HDPE

### Pool/Spa Plumbing

#### Required Features
- **Main drain covers**: VGB Act compliant
- **Suction entrapment**: Dual drains or safety system
- **SVRS**: Safety vacuum release system if single drain
- **Backflow**: Required on fill line

#### Equipment Pad
- **Location**: Accessible for service
- **Clearance**: Per manufacturer
- **Drainage**: Proper slope away

### Reclaimed Water Systems

#### Cross-Connection Prevention
- **Color coding**: Purple pipe required
- **Labeling**: "RECLAIMED WATER - DO NOT DRINK"
- **Separate trenching**: From potable supply
- **No interior connections**: Exterior irrigation only

### Septic Systems (Florida)
- **Permit required**: Through DOH (Department of Health)
- **Setbacks**: Per county regulations
- **Soil testing**: Required
- **Tank sizing**: Based on bedrooms
