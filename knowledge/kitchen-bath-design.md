# Kitchen and Bathroom Design

## Kitchen Layout Principles

### Work Triangle
```
Refrigerator → Sink → Range

Rules:
- Each leg: 4' to 9'
- Total triangle: 13' to 26'
- No traffic through triangle
- No obstacles crossing legs
```

### Kitchen Layouts
| Layout | Min Size | Best For |
|--------|----------|----------|
| Galley | 8' × 10' | Apartments, small spaces |
| L-shaped | 10' × 10' | Open to living area |
| U-shaped | 10' × 12' | Serious cooks |
| G-shaped | 10' × 12' | Maximum storage |
| Island | 12' × 14' min | Entertaining, multiple cooks |

### Counter Space Requirements
| Location | Min Length |
|----------|------------|
| Each side of sink | 24" |
| Each side of range | 15" (one side 24") |
| Near refrigerator | 15" |
| Total countertop | 158" for one cook |

## Kitchen Electrical

### Required Outlets
| Location | Requirement |
|----------|-------------|
| Counter (every 4') | 20A GFCI, at least 2 circuits |
| Island | 1 outlet minimum |
| Refrigerator | Dedicated 20A circuit |
| Dishwasher | Dedicated 20A circuit |
| Microwave | Dedicated 20A circuit |
| Range (electric) | Dedicated 50A circuit |
| Range (gas) | 120V for ignition |
| Disposal | 20A, switched or air switch |

### Outlet Placement
- 6" to 12" above counter
- No more than 24" from counter edge to outlet
- Within 4' of any point on counter
- Not directly above range or sink

## Accessible Bathroom Design

### Clear Floor Space
- 60" turning radius or T-turn
- 30" × 48" at each fixture
- Clear floor space may overlap

### Toilet Requirements
- 17"-19" seat height (comfort height)
- 18" from side wall to centerline
- 42" grab bar on side wall
- 36" grab bar behind toilet
- 60" from side wall to other obstruction

### Roll-in Shower Requirements
- 36" × 60" minimum (30" × 60" alternate)
- No threshold (or 1/2" max)
- Fold-down seat
- Grab bars on three walls
- Handheld showerhead on slide bar

### Lavatory Requirements
- 34" max height
- Knee clearance: 27"H × 30"W × 19"D
- Insulated pipes
- Lever handles or motion sensor

## Bathroom Waterproofing

### Shower/Tub Surround
```
Layers (from structure out):
1. Framing
2. Cement board (not green board)
3. Waterproof membrane
4. Thin-set mortar
5. Tile
```

### Waterproof Membrane Options
| Type | Application | Cost |
|------|-------------|------|
| Sheet membrane | Bonded to substrate | $$$ |
| Liquid applied | Rolled/brushed on | $$ |
| Fabric membrane | Embedded in thin-set | $$ |
| Foam board | Pre-made shower pan | $$$ |

### Critical Details
- Pan membrane up walls min 6"
- Shower dam/curb waterproofed
- Corners and transitions reinforced
- Slope to drain: 1/4" per foot
- Weep holes in clamping ring

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

### Kitchen
- Not enough counter space
- Refrigerator door blocking traffic
- Dishwasher too far from sink
- No landing space at appliances
- Island too large or too small
- Inadequate electrical
- Hood venting undersized

### Bathroom
- Door swings into fixture
- Toilet visible from door
- No storage
- Inadequate ventilation
- Non-waterproof materials in wet areas
- Insufficient lighting at mirror
- No accessible outlet for hair dryer
