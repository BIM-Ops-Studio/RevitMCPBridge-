# Material Selection Guide - Decision Logic

## Exterior Cladding Selection

### Decision Tree
```
What is the project type?
  Residential (1-3 story) → Go to Residential Options
  Commercial/Multi-family → Go to Commercial Options

What is the climate?
  Florida Coastal → Impact rated, salt resistant
  Hurricane Zone → Wind-rated assemblies
  High UV → Fade-resistant materials

What is the budget?
  Economy → Vinyl siding, stucco
  Mid-range → Fiber cement, metal panel
  Premium → Brick, stone, high-end metal
```

### Residential Cladding Options

| Material | Cost ($/SF) | Lifespan | Maintenance | Best For |
|----------|-------------|----------|-------------|----------|
| Vinyl siding | $3-8 | 20-40 yrs | Low | Budget residential |
| Fiber cement (Hardie) | $6-12 | 30-50 yrs | Low | Mid-range, all climates |
| Stucco (3-coat) | $6-9 | 50+ yrs | Medium | Florida, Southwest |
| Wood siding | $8-15 | 15-30 yrs | High | Traditional aesthetic |
| Brick veneer | $12-25 | 100+ yrs | Very low | Premium, traditional |
| Stone veneer | $15-35 | 50+ yrs | Low | Accent areas |
| Metal panel | $10-25 | 40-60 yrs | Low | Modern aesthetic |

### Commercial Cladding Options

| Material | Cost ($/SF) | Fire Rating | Best For |
|----------|-------------|-------------|----------|
| Metal panel | $12-30 | Varies | Modern, industrial |
| EIFS | $8-15 | 1-hr possible | Commercial, cost-effective |
| Curtain wall | $50-150 | 1-hr possible | High-rise, office |
| Precast concrete | $30-60 | 2-4 hr | Parking, industrial |
| Masonry (CMU) | $15-25 | 2-4 hr | Institutional, retail |
| Terracotta | $40-80 | 2-hr+ | High-end commercial |

### Florida Coastal Requirements
Must consider:
- Miami-Dade NOA (Notice of Acceptance) if in HVHZ
- Impact resistance within 30' of grade
- Wind-driven rain resistance
- Salt spray corrosion resistance
- UV stability

## Flooring Selection

### Decision Tree
```
What is the use?
  Residential living areas → Wood, LVP, carpet
  Commercial high traffic → Polished concrete, VCT, porcelain
  Wet areas → Porcelain tile, sheet vinyl
  Industrial → Sealed concrete, epoxy

What is the maintenance tolerance?
  Low → Porcelain, polished concrete, LVP
  Medium → Hardwood, carpet tile
  High → Real wood, natural stone, carpet

What is the budget?
  Economy → VCT, carpet, sheet vinyl
  Mid-range → LVP, engineered wood, ceramic
  Premium → Hardwood, porcelain, natural stone
```

| VCT | $2-4 | 15-25 yrs | Limited | Commercial, institutional |
| LVP/LVT | $4-10 | 15-25 yrs | Yes | Residential, light commercial |

### Subfloor Requirements

| Flooring | Subfloor Prep |
|----------|---------------|
| Tile | Flat within 1/4" per 10', cement board or concrete |
| LVP | Flat within 3/16" per 10', smooth surface |
| Hardwood | Plywood, moisture barrier if over concrete |
| Carpet | Pad required, smooth subfloor |
| VCT | Very smooth, level surface |

## Window Selection

### Decision Tree
```
What is the climate?
  Florida → Impact-rated required in HVHZ
  Cold climate → Low-E, triple pane
  Hot climate → Low-E, solar control

What is the style?
  Traditional → Double-hung, casement
  Modern → Fixed, large panes
  Industrial → Steel, aluminum

What is the budget?
  Economy → Vinyl, double pane
  Mid-range → Fiberglass, aluminum with thermal break
  Premium → Wood, aluminum-clad wood
```

### Window Frame Materials

| Material | Cost | Maintenance | Insulation | Best For |
|----------|------|-------------|------------|----------|
| Vinyl | $ | Very low | Good | Budget, all climates |
| Aluminum | $$ | Low | Poor | Modern, commercial |
| Aluminum (thermal break) | $$$ | Low | Good | Modern, all climates |
| Fiberglass | $$$ | Very low | Excellent | All climates |
| Wood | $$$$ | High | Excellent | Traditional |
| Aluminum-clad wood | $$$$ | Medium | Excellent | Premium traditional |

### Glass Options

| Type | Purpose | U-Factor | SHGC |
|------|---------|----------|------|
| Clear double | Basic | 0.48 | 0.70 |
| Low-E | Energy efficiency | 0.30 | 0.25-0.40 |
| Low-E + Argon | Enhanced efficiency | 0.26 | 0.25-0.40 |
| Triple pane | Cold climates | 0.18-0.22 | 0.25-0.35 |
| Impact | Hurricane zones | Varies | Varies |
| Tinted | Glare/solar control | Varies | 0.40-0.60 |

### Florida/HVHZ Requirements
- Large missile impact (9 lb 2x4 at 50 fps)
- Small missile impact (2g steel balls)
- Cyclic pressure testing
- NOA (Notice of Acceptance) required
- Labels must remain visible after installation
