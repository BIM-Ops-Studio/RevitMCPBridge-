## FULL NARRATION SCRIPT (for recording)

**[INTRO - 5 seconds silence with music]**

What if your AI assistant... could actually draw construction documents?

Every day, architects around the world sit down at their computers. And for most of them, roughly 65% of their time goes to one thing: documentation. Not designing. Not problem-solving. Repetitive, tedious documentation.

A typical commercial project? Fifty to a hundred sheets. Floor plans, elevations, sections, details, schedules. Each one placed, dimensioned, annotated by hand. It takes weeks. Sometimes months.

AI can write code. Generate art. Compose music. Analyze legal documents. Design websites. But ask Claude, GPT, or any AI to create a Revit floor plan? To place walls, doors, dimensions? Nothing. That's the gap.

On one side: AI. Natural language, flexible, conversational. On the other: Revit. Precise geometry, complex parameters, forty thousand API methods. These two worlds were never designed to communicate.

What if we could build a bridge?

The vision: A direct connection. You tell Claude what you want—in plain English. 'Create a twenty by thirty foot room. Add two doors and three windows.' And it happens. Instantly. In Revit. No mouse. No menus. Just... intent to reality.

We're BIM Ops Studio, based in South Florida. For years, we've produced construction documents for multi-family, single-family, and commercial projects. We know the pain. We live it every day. So we decided to fix it.

We call it RevitMCPBridge. MCP—Model Context Protocol—is how Claude talks to external tools. Our bridge takes that protocol and connects it directly to Revit. Both 2025 and 2026 versions. Proprietary technology. Production-ready.

In this series, we'll take you through the journey. From the first lines of code to seven hundred five working methods. From manual wall creation to autonomous construction document production. This is that story.

Next episode: Building the Bridge. How we connected two worlds that were never meant to talk.
